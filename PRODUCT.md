# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Wedding guests — friends and family of Nidhi Kesarkar (Birmingham, UK) and Parag Khalde (Mumbai, India). Many guests are Indian diaspora or based in India; some are UK-side family and friends unfamiliar with the venue city. They arrive via a personal link, typically on mobile. Their job is to feel the occasion, confirm attendance, and find practical details (venue, travel, menu). The emotional job is equally important: they should feel the intimacy and scale of this specific love story, not a generic wedding.

## Product Purpose

A digital wedding invitation for a Hindu Maharashtrian wedding on December 4, 2026 at Abhishek Farms, Yeoor Hills, Thane — a floating mandap in a pool. The site tells the couple's story (long-distance Birmingham–Mumbai, a shower proposal, first meeting at Runwal Greens), collects RSVPs, shares the menu and schedule, and has a trivia game for guests. Success means a guest closes the tab feeling they already know Nidhi and Parag, not just where to show up.

## Brand Personality

Intimate · Cinematic · Indian

Voice is warm and personal — specific, not generic. The couple's story has real detail (wallet found on the street, asked out in a shower, long-distance from September 2025) and the site should honour that specificity. Tone is celebratory but never loud. Copy rewards close reading with small wit ("not directed by James Cameron").

## Anti-references

Avoid: all-white, cold editorial minimalism — the kind of luxury that signals restraint over warmth. This wedding has Indian cultural identity and a love story with real texture; stripping it to cool geometry would erase that.

## Design Principles

1. **Warmth over restraint** — The gold-on-ivory palette and glass-gold effects signal a room lit by candlelight, not a gallery. Every screen should feel like it's glowing from within.
2. **The moment, not the logistics** — Lead with emotion. The love story, the photo, the countdown come before venue coordinates. Information earns its place after feeling.
3. **Indian identity as anchor, not ornament** — The lotus, jali pattern, and warm gold are cultural signals that belong here authentically. They are not decorative novelty applied over a Western template.
4. **Cinematic restraint** — Full-screen parallax, slow rotations, dramatic overlays — but always slowing down, never cluttering. One thing moves at a time.
5. **Personal over polished** — The shower proposal and the Birmingham–Mumbai distance are more powerful than any stock-photo romance. Specificity beats generic elegance.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast minimum for all text on overlays. `prefers-reduced-motion` respected on decorative animations (lotus rotation, particles). Touch targets 44px minimum on mobile. The intro screen map interaction has a tap fallback for users who cannot drag.
