## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**Does this look AI-generated?**

**LLM assessment**: No — this does not read as AI-generated. The specificity is what saves it: the shower proposal, the wallet story, the "not directed by James Cameron" subtitle, the Jab We Met Bollywood reference. These are details that only a real couple has, and they're woven into the UI rather than bolted on. The jali background pattern, lotus ornaments, and floating mandap concept are culturally grounded, not aesthetic novelty. The warmth of the copy and the restraint of the palette (gold used sparingly, ivory body, near-invisible texture) feel intentional rather than templated.

**That said**, one absolute ban is present: the hero names ("Nidhi" and "Parag") use `background-clip: text` with a gold shimmer gradient (`.text-gold-shimmer` class, `app/globals.css:80`). The impeccable skill classifies gradient text as an absolute ban — "decorative, never meaningful." Here it's genuinely arguable: the shimmer on the names is the central visual moment and has precedent in Indian wedding aesthetics (gold foil, metallic letterpress). I'm flagging it rather than demanding removal — but it's worth considering whether a single solid gold `#C49A28` with the same `clamp(48px, 11vw, 92px)` and weight 100 would be as powerful and cleaner.

**Deterministic scan**: 13 warnings, 111 advisories.

- **1 genuine warning**: `admin/page.tsx:80` — `from-purple-600 to-blue-500` gradient on the admin login screen. Classic AI color palette. Completely off-brand; only Parag sees it, but still.
- **5 bounce-easing warnings**: `IntroScreen.module.css:156`, `Poll.tsx:108`, `Poll.tsx:147`, `games/page.tsx:151`, `games/page.tsx:162`, `games/page.tsx:458` — all use `cubic-bezier(0.34, 1.56, 0.64, 1)` (spring overshoot) or `animate-bounce`. DESIGN.md's Cinematic Restraint principle says ease-out only, no bounce.
- **5 layout-transition warnings**: `Poll.tsx` and `games/page.tsx` — `transition: width` on poll bar widths. Layout property transitions can cause jank on low-end Android devices common in the Indian market.
- **111 design-system-color advisories**: Largely false positives — these are one-off rgba transparency values, gradient intermediate stops, and overlay colors that don't need to be in the token system. The two substantive ones: `#B8850A` and `#E8C547` (gradient stops on the primary button) should be documented in DESIGN.md as gradient endpoints under a `gold-dark` and `gold-bright` token.

No false positives among the 13 warnings.

## Overall Impression

This is a genuinely good wedding invitation — personal, warm, and culturally specific. The visual system is cohesive and the copy lands consistently. The biggest risk is not aesthetics; it's access. The intro screen drag interaction is the gatekeeper to the entire site, and there's no escape hatch for users who can't figure it out (elderly relatives, motor-impaired users, confused first-timers). That is the single change that most improves the site before invitations go out.

## What's Working

**The copy throughout.** "Count me in!", "A little note for the couple 💌", "Single, tho looking for someone", "not directed by James Cameron" — the voice is consistent, specific, and rewards reading. This is genuinely hard to achieve.

**FloatingRSVPButton.tsx.** Well-architected: appears only after hero scrolls away, hides when RSVP is in view, respects lightbox and menu state, safe area insets, correct thumb-zone placement. This is the kind of detail that actually ships.

**The asymmetric gallery grid.** The editorial 5+4+3 column layout with hover captions and the lightbox is the strongest section. It avoids the identical-card-grid ban entirely and feels curated rather than generated.

## Priority Issues

### [P1] Intro screen has no skip or tap fallback

**What**: The Leaflet map drag-to-unlock is the only path into the site. There is no skip button, no tap/click fallback, and no double-tap override. The `sessionStorage` bypass (`invite_unlocked: true`) only works for developers.

**Why it matters**: PRODUCT.md explicitly notes "the intro screen map interaction has a tap fallback for users who cannot drag" — but no such fallback exists in the code. This will fail for: elderly family members unfamiliar with drag gestures, users with motor impairments, iOS users where Leaflet pointer events occasionally misbehave, and any user on a slow connection where the map tiles don't load fully before they try to interact. For a wedding invitation, the audience skews wide in age and device capability.

**Fix**: Add a "Skip intro →" link below the instruction bar, styled subtly (gold, small, tracking-widest). Alternatively, add a `triggerUnlock()` call on double-tap of the map overlay. The skip link is simpler and more universally accessible.

**Suggested command**: `/impeccable harden intro screen`

### [P1] Gradient text on couple's names (absolute ban)

**What**: `app/globals.css:80-93` — `.text-gold-shimmer` uses `background: linear-gradient(90deg, ...)` + `background-clip: text` + `-webkit-text-fill-color: transparent`. Applied to both names in the hero at `clamp(48px, 11vw, 92px)`.

**Why it matters**: `background-clip: text` is on the impeccable absolute bans list — it's decorative, screen-reader-incompatible in some contexts, and renders poorly at high DPI scaling on older devices. More importantly: the shimmer animation runs continuously at 4s loop, consuming battery on mobile.

**Fix**: Replace with solid `color: #C49A28` (Temple Gold). The names will retain their weight, size, and impact. If the gold feels flat without the shimmer, add a CSS `text-shadow: 0 0 40px rgba(196,154,40,0.25)` to give warmth without the gradient-clip technique.

**Suggested command**: `/impeccable polish hero`

---

### [P2] Bounce easing throughout games and poll pages

**What**: `games/page.tsx:151,162,458` and `Poll.tsx:108,147` use `cubic-bezier(0.34, 1.56, 0.64, 1)` — a spring overshoot curve. `GuestBook.tsx:87` uses Tailwind's `animate-bounce` on the loading dots.

**Why it matters**: The DESIGN.md principle "Cinematic Restraint — always slowing down, never cluttering" is violated. The overshoot on poll bar width animations and vote confirmation cards creates a playful, bouncy feel that's tonally mismatched with the ceremony aesthetic established on the main page. The games page is lighter in tone, but "elastic" is different from "light."

**Fix**: Replace `cubic-bezier(0.34, 1.56, 0.64, 1)` with `cubic-bezier(0.25, 0.46, 0.45, 0.94)` (standard ease) or `cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)` (ease-out-expo). Replace `animate-bounce` with three pulsing opacity dots (no vertical movement).

**Suggested command**: `/impeccable animate games page`

---

### [P2] Admin login page is completely off-brand

**What**: `app/admin/page.tsx:80` — the login screen uses `bg-gradient-to-br from-purple-600 to-blue-500 flex items-center justify-center`. The input field uses `focus:ring-purple-400`.

**Why it matters**: The admin page is where Parag checks RSVPs and monitors attendance. It should feel like part of the same site. Purple/blue on a warm-gold wedding invitation site reads as "forgot to change the Tailwind starter." It's the AI color palette flag at its most literal.

**Fix**: Replace `from-purple-600 to-blue-500` with a dark warm background (`#0D0600` or `#1A0A00`). Replace `ring-purple-400` with `ring-[#C49A28]`. Apply the `glass-gold` card style to the login panel. Two minutes of work; completely on-brand.

**Suggested command**: `/impeccable polish admin`

---

### [P3] Menu page has placeholder content

**What**: `app/menu/page.tsx` — all three sections (On Arrival, Main Course, Desserts) contain placeholder item names and descriptions.

**Why it matters**: The Menu link is in the live nav. Guests clicking it before the real menu is loaded will see fabricated dishes. For a December 2026 wedding, the real menu likely won't be confirmed until closer to the date — but having a visible nav link leading to placeholder content undermines trust.

**Fix**: Either hide the Menu nav link until real content is ready, or add a "Menu coming soon — check back closer to the date!" state when the array is empty/placeholder-flagged.

**Suggested command**: `/impeccable harden menu`

## Persona Red Flags

**Jordan (Confused First-Timer)**: Opens the invite URL on mobile. Sees a dark map with two city markers and a plane. The instruction bar says "Drag the plane from Birmingham to Mumbai" — but they've never heard of this interaction pattern. They tap the plane. Nothing. They tap Mumbai. Nothing. They zoom the map trying to find something to do. After 15 seconds they may abandon entirely. **The intro screen is the single biggest Jordan failure mode.** If one of Parag's or Nidhi's older relatives can't get past the intro, they will think the site is broken, not that they need to drag.

**Casey (Distracted Mobile User)**: Opens on Android while commuting. Sees the map, tries to interact, gets redirected to /games after RSVP with only 2.5s to react. If she switches apps mid-RSVP, she returns to the form blank (no state persistence). The 100-word textarea requires typing — on mobile, autocorrect may mangle names and message copy. The RSVP confirmation text ("You're on the list! See you December 4th 🎉") appears only in the toast which auto-dismisses. Casey may not realize she successfully RSVPed. **Add a persistent success state** (not just a toast) showing the name and date confirmed.

**Pachi / Mama (The Bharat-based Relative)**: Profile: 55–65-year-old relative in India/UK. Received the link on WhatsApp. Mid-range Android device (Redmi, Moto). Comfortable with Facebook but not "website interactions." Reads Marathi first, English second. **Specific red flags**: (1) The map interaction is the hardest thing they've ever been asked to do on a phone. No cultural frame for "drag a plane on a map." (2) The "Trivia" nav link — they won't know what this is. (3) The word "RSVP" — this is English jargon; the question "Will you be there?" is better (and is already the section heading). (4) If the intro screen fails for them, there is no phone number to call for help visible without getting past the intro.

## Minor Observations

- `#B8850A` and `#E8C547` (gradient stop colours in FloatingRSVPButton and the primary button) are undocumented in DESIGN.md. Should be added as `gold-dark` and `gold-bright` token entries.
- `IntroScreen.module.css:214,251,274` — `border-radius: 20px` and `24px` are outside the DESIGN.md rounded scale (the scale goes `sm:6, md:8, xl:12, 2xl:16, full:100`). Adding `3xl: 20px` and `4xl: 24px` would document rather than change these.
- The Gallery section `meta` property (e.g., "Birmingham") is rendered but isn't visually prominent enough to read at a glance on mobile — it overlaps with the caption on hover.
- The `animate-bounce` Tailwind class on the GuestBook loading dots also triggers on `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` — Tailwind's built-in `motion-safe:` prefix isn't applied. This means users who have motion disabled see bouncing dots.
- The long-distance photo caption ("Long distance started Sept 23 2025") uses the date Sept 23 — CLAUDE.md says September 2025 but doesn't specify day. Worth confirming.
